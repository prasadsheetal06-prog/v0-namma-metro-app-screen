Last mile integration via Namma Yatri and ONDC
Namma Yatri is built on the Open Network for Digital Commerce (ONDC), a government-backed open protocol. Fare estimates are publicly accessible, making it the only ride-hailing platform suitable for direct integration with a government transit app. Rapido and Ola do not expose public fare APIs.

Key Product Metrics and KPIs
SMART framework — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Each metric maps directly to a validated problem in the current Namma Metro app.
KPI 01
Google Maps Fallback Rate
Specific% of active sessions where user opens Google Maps during or after using the Namma Metro app
MeasurableTracked via app exit event + Google Maps deeplink trigger rate
AchievableBaseline 66%. Target: below 25%
RelevantDirectly measures the core gap — the app not being enough on its own
Time-boundMeasured at 30 and 60 days post-launch
KPI 02
Journey Completion Rate
Specific% of Plan Journey sessions that result in a QR ticket purchase within the same session
MeasurableFunnel tracking from Plan Journey entry to ticket purchase confirmation
AchievableTarget: 70% completion rate within 4 weeks of launch
RelevantUsers plan but do not book = feature adds friction. This is the core conversion check.
Time-boundReviewed weekly for first 4 weeks, then monthly
KPI 03
Navigation Error Rate
Specific% of users who report wrong platform, wrong exit, or missed interchange in post-trip feedback
MeasurableIn-app post-journey one-tap rating: Was your journey smooth? Yes or No
AchievableBaseline 86% experienced issues. Target: below 30% within 90 days
RelevantMaps directly to the most reported pain point — physical navigation failure at the station
Time-boundTracked continuously from day one, reviewed at 30, 60, and 90 day marks
KPI 04
7-Day Return Rate
Specific% of users who used Plan Journey at least once and returned within the next 7 days
MeasurableCohort analysis from first Plan Journey use, tracked per user ID
AchievableCurrent WAU baseline is 10%. Target: 60% for Plan Journey users within 90 days
RelevantDaily commuters should return daily. This measures habit formation, the app's weakest current metric.
Time-boundMeasured at 7, 14, and 30 day cohort windows from first use
KPI 05
Plan Journey Session Length
SpecificAverage time from tapping Plan Journey to completing the route plan and seeing all 6 steps
MeasurableSession timer from screen entry to scroll completion of final card
AchievableTarget: 45 to 90 seconds. Under 45 = not engaging. Over 90 = confused.
RelevantMeasures usability and information clarity — key for a first-time-user feature at a busy station
Time-boundBaseline from first 2 weeks of launch. Optimised if outside target range by week 4.
KPI 06
Feature Adoption Rate
Specific% of monthly active Namma Metro users who have tapped Plan Journey at least once
MeasurableUnique user count on Plan Journey tile tap divided by total MAU
AchievableTarget: 40% of MAU try the feature within first 30 days of launch
RelevantA feature only 5% of users find is not solving a problem. Adoption rate validates feature discoverability.
Time-boundTracked daily for first 30 days, reviewed at end of month 1 and month 3
